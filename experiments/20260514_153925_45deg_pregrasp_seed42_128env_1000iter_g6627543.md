## Evaluation

This run confirms that the deterministic blue pregrasp waypoint is being used by the reward signal, because distance metrics improved substantially. However, the reward shaping is not yet forcing the correct gripper orientation. The policy learned to reduce distance while pointing the gripper almost opposite the desired 45-degree insertion direction.

The most likely issue is the relationship between `gripper_forward_axis_b` and `approach_dir`. Either the sign of the forward axis is opposite the intended physical gripper direction, or the alignment reward is too weak and too strongly gated by pregrasp distance. The next change should fix that before running another full baseline.

## Recommended Next Fix

1. Visually confirm whether local `+X` or local `-X` is the actual gripper insertion direction.
2. If needed, flip `gripper_forward_axis_b` or change the alignment calculation to compare against `-approach_dir`.
3. Add a small always-on alignment shaping term so the policy does not learn the wrong orientation before reaching the blue waypoint.
4. Relax `pregrasp_success_radius` temporarily from `0.050` to about `0.080` for early educational experiments, then tighten it after the behavior appears.
5. Re-run `train_visual_16_300.sh --seed 42` before another full `train_128_1000.sh`.
